  <a id="12014" class="Comment">-- What&#39;s the difference between m&gt;=n and m &gt;= n ?</a>
     <a id="12070" class="Comment">{- m&gt;=n (without spaces) is just an identifier; it could be anything,
        but it has been chosen to be suggestive of its *type* which is
        m &gt;= n (with spaces) which is the proposition that m is at least n.
        By &quot;proposition&quot;, I mean &quot;type with at most one inhabitant&quot;, where
        we care more about whether there is an inhabitant or not than which
        one (because there&#39;s never a choice). Finished code does not show
        us the types of its components, and that&#39;s not always a good thing.
        Here, by picking nice names, we get something of an aide-memoire. -}</a>

  <a id="12668" class="Comment">-- What does (x ,-_) mean?</a>
     <a id="12700" class="Comment">{- It&#39;s a &quot;left section&quot;. Right sections (_,- xs) also exist sometimes.
        Why only sometimes? -}</a>

  <a id="12806" class="Comment">-- &quot;Why is it stuck?&quot;</a>
     <a id="12833" class="Comment">{- Proof by induction isn&#39;t just flailing about, you know? The trick is
        to pick the case analysis that provokes the &quot;stuck&quot; programs to do a
        step of computation. Then the same reasoning that justifies the
        termination of the program will justify the induction in a proof
        about it. -}</a>{% endraw %}</pre>
