SAT solvers

This will contain some thoughts about sat solving. See the references.

  • Many SATs solvers are based on Davis-Putnam-Logemann-Loveland (DPLL) procedure.
  • The improvements in these programs are mostly:
    • better implementation techniques, such as the two-watched literal approach for unit propagation
    • several conceptual enhancements on the original DPLL procedure, aimed at reducing the amount of explored search space such as backjumping (a form of non-chronological backtracking), conflict-driven lemma learning, and restarts.
    • they can solve problems with even tens of thousands of variables and millions of clauses.
  • Nieuwenhuis, R., Nieuwenhuis, R., Oliveras, A., Oliveras, A., Tinelli, C., & Tinelli, C. (2006). Solving SAT and SAT Modulo Theories: From an Abstract Davis–Putnam–Logemann–Loveland Procedure to DPLL(T). J. Acm, 53(6), 937–977. https://doi.org/10.1145/1217856.1217859
